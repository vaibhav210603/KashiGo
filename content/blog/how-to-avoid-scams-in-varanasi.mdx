# The Real Varanasi: Beyond the Touts

Varanasi is the heart of India. It’s a city of light, of death, and of an energy you won't find anywhere else on Earth. But let’s be honest: for a first-time foreign visitor, it can also be a city of intense frustration. 

I was born in the narrow galis (lanes) of Kashi. I’ve seen the city change, but one thing remains constant: the "Tourist Economy." Because Varanasi is so disorienting, touts and scammers thrive on your confusion. 

Here are the 9 major scams you will face, and exactly how to handle them like a local.

## 1. The "Burning Ghat" Donation Scam
This is the most sensitive and aggressive scam in the city. It happens at Manikarnika Ghat (the main cremation ghat).
**The Scam:** A "helpful" local or a "holy man" will approach you, offer to explain the cremation process for free, and then lead you to a balcony. They will tell you about poor families who can't afford wood for their loved ones. They will then demand a "donation" for the wood, often starting at $50 or $100.
**The Truth:** You do NOT need to pay anyone to watch or walk through the ghats. No legitimate priest or family member will ask a tourist for wood money. 
**How to Avoid:** If someone starts explaining the ghat to you uninvited, say "No thank you, I'm just observing" and keep walking. If they persist, walk away toward the main riverfront.

## 2. The Fake Guide / "Free Tour"
**The Scam:** Someone approaches you near Dashashwamedh Ghat or in the lanes. They speak excellent English and say, "I am a student, I just want to practice my English. I will show you the real hidden Varanasi for free."
**The Truth:** They will inevitably lead you to a silk shop or a tea shop where they get a 30-50% commission on anything you buy. You will pay double the price for low-quality goods.
**How to Avoid:** There is no such thing as a free tour from a stranger in the galis. Politely decline and use a reputable booking platform or your hotel's services.

## 3. The Boat Ride Price Inflation
**The Scam:** You walk to the ghat and a boatman quotes you INR 3,000 for a one-hour sunrise ride. 
**The Truth:** The fair price for a private boat for 2-4 people is between INR 600 and INR 1,500 depending on the season and your negotiation skills. 
**How to Avoid:** Never board a boat without fixing the price and the duration. Or, better yet, [book through KashiGo](/book) where the price is fixed and fair.

## 4. The Fake Holy Man (Sadhus)
**The Scam:** Men dressed in bright orange with painted faces will beckon you for a photo. After you click, they will demand INR 500-1,000.
**The Truth:** Real Sadhus rarely ask for money for photos. The "photo sadhus" are often just people in costume looking for tourist dollars.
**How to Avoid:** If you want a photo, ask first and offer a small amount (INR 20-50) or just walk past.

## 5. The Silk Shop Commission Trap
**The Scam:** Your rickshaw driver says, "The temple is closed for an hour, let me take you to a government silk factory while you wait."
**The Truth:** The temple is not closed. He is taking you to a shop that pays him for every tourist he brings in.
**How to Avoid:** Stick to your itinerary. Tell the driver, "No shops, just the temple."

## 6. The "Wood Donation" at Manikarnika (Again)
Seriously, this is so common it deserves two mentions. They might even show you fake ledgers of other tourists who "donated" large amounts. It's all fake.

## 7. The ATM Skimming / Card Swap
**The Scam:** A "helpful" person at an ATM says the machine is tricky and offers to help you insert your card.
**The Truth:** They are trying to see your PIN or swap your card for a fake one.
**How to Avoid:** Never let anyone help you at an ATM. Cover your PIN with your hand.

## 8. The "Broken Rickshaw" / Detour
**The Scam:** Halfway to your destination, the driver says his rickshaw is broken or the road is blocked by a "festival." He takes you to a different hotel or shop instead.
**The Truth:** He wants the commission from the other place.
**How to Avoid:** Use Google Maps. If he takes a wrong turn, tell him to stop and get out.

## 9. The "Foreigner Tax"
**The Scam:** Shops near the ghats won't have price tags. They see your face and double the price.
**The Truth:** Most items have an MRP (Maximum Retail Price) printed on them in India.
**How to Avoid:** Look for the MRP on packaged goods. For handicrafts, you must negotiate or buy from fixed-price boutiques.
